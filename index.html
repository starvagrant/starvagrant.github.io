After The Hack Night

Turning Hack Nights to Projects. Coders code, and the best coders collaborate. In teaching myself to program, there's always been one thing that's frustrating. I can't find an open source project to work on. The programs I love to use? They're already so complicated I'll never figure out their internals. And searching for something small through github? It doesn't look to promising. But plenty of cities have meetups, ala Meetup.com, the do have regular hack nights. What if that could make it's way into a repository to work on at a later date. Currently, I'm looking of doing something specific for the KC PHP Users Group, but a good test case could lead to something more general. After the Hack Night

Open Rpg

Coders love games. Nothing gets the mind ready for real life like simulated life, provided you wanted to actually live. In this repo I take on two challenges: one, an rpg tracking system for tabletop games (quite in it's infancy) and a card game in the works called Super PAC, where players get to enjoy all the fun of political scheming without the banal cheerleading of real electoral politics.
